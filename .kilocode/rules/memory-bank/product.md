# Dreptuś Product Description

## Why This Project Exists
Dreptuś was created to solve the problem of promoting outdoor tourism and family recreation in Poland. Traditional tourism often lacks engaging, interactive elements that encourage families to explore their local environment and discover new places together.

## Problems It Solves

### 1. Lack of Structured Outdoor Activities
- **Problem**: Families struggle to find organized, meaningful outdoor activities that are both educational and entertaining
- **Solution**: Provides pre-designed route maps with check-point challenges that combine navigation skills with local history learning

### 2. Disconnected Tourism Experience
- **Problem**: Traditional tourism experiences are often passive (just visiting places)
- **Solution**: Creates an active, participatory experience where users must complete tasks and engage with locations

### 3. Missing Achievement Framework
- **Problem**: No recognition system for outdoor activity completion
- **Solution**: Badge-based achievement system that gamifies tourism and provides incentive for continued participation

### 4. Difficulty in Route Discovery
- **Problem**: Finding well-designed, family-friendly routes is challenging
- **Solution**: Centralized platform with filtering, mapping, and detailed route information

### 5. Limited Tourism Data Collection
- **Problem**: No system to track participation in outdoor tourism activities
- **Solution**: Complete participant tracking system that can provide insights into tourism patterns

## How It Should Work

### For General Users:
1. **Browse Routes**: Visit the website to discover available travel routes
2. **Filter & Select**: Use location-based filtering to find routes in their area
3. **Download Materials**: Access route descriptions, maps, and instructions
4. **Complete Route**: Follow the route at their own pace, completing check-point tasks
5. **Submit Report**: Use the online form to report route completion with answers to check-point questions
6. **Earn Badges**: Receive achievement badges for completed routes and participate in higher-level challenges

### For Administrators:
1. **Manage Content**: Create and edit route information in the WordPress backend
2. **Track Participants**: Monitor submitted reports and verify completion
3. **Issue Badges**: Manage badge distribution based on completion criteria
4. **Analyze Data**: Review participation statistics and route popularity
5. **Moderate Submissions**: Review and approve participant reports

### For Participants:
1. **Create Account**: Register to access the platform (optional)
2. **Join Community**: Participate in outdoor tourism community
3. **Track Progress**: Monitor their own route completion history
4. **Share Experience**: Connect with other participants and share experiences

## User Experience Goals

### Accessibility
- Simple, intuitive interface that works on all devices
- Clear navigation and route information display
- Mobile-optimized for use during actual route completion

### Engagement
- Visual progress tracking and achievement displays
- Community features to encourage participation
- Regular content updates to maintain interest

### Educational Value
- Each route includes historical and geographical information
- Check-point tasks encourage learning about local culture and nature
- Progressive difficulty levels to accommodate different skill levels

### Gamification
- Clear achievement criteria and badge visualization
- Progress tracking and completion statistics
- Community leaderboards and recognition systems

## User Journey Examples

### New User Journey:
1. User visits dreptuś.pl and learns about the TRIP concept
2. Browses available routes using the interactive map
3. Selects a local route and downloads/prints materials
4. Completes the route over a weekend with family
5. Submits completion report online
6. Receives confirmation and progress tracking
7. Discovers more routes in their region

### Regular Participant Journey:
1. Checks new routes added to the platform
2. Plans longer trips to collect specific badges
3. Uses admin portal to track community-wide statistics
4. Provides feedback on routes and suggests improvements

### Administrator Journey:
1. Reviews new route submissions from field teams
2. Processes participant reports and verifies completion
3. Issues badges based on completion criteria
4. Analyzes platform statistics to guide content strategy
5. Manages user community and addresses questions

## Success Metrics & KPIs
- Route completion rate (reports submitted vs. downloads)
- Badge distribution across categories
- User engagement time on the platform
- Geographic coverage of available routes
- Community growth and activity levels
